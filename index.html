Historical Kasbah Houses

Come be apart of history

Kasbahs are fortified structures or citadels found primarily in North Africa, especially Morocco. They were built by the Amazigh (Berber) people using materials like clay and stone. Historically, kasbahs served as defensive strongholds and residences for powerful families or tribal leaders. Today, many have been converted into hotels or tourist attractions, showcasing their rich architectural and cultural heritage.

Explore all of our locations

Tetouan, often referred to as the “White Dove of Morocco,” is a city that beautifully blends history, culture, and stunning landscapes. Nestled at the base of the Rif Mountains and just a short distance from the Mediterranean Sea, Tetouan offers a unique charm that captivates every visitor.

The Ouzoud Waterfalls, located near Marrakech, are the highest in North Africa, cascading 110 meters (360 feet) through a stunning red-rock gorge12. This natural wonder is perfect for a refreshing day trip, offering breathtaking views and a chance to escape the city’s hustle and bustle.

Rabat, the capital city of Morocco, is a captivating blend of historical charm and modern sophistication. Located on the Atlantic coast at the mouth of the Bou Regreg River, Rabat is known for its stunning architecture, lush gardens, and vibrant cultural scene.

Agadir, located on Morocco’s southern Atlantic coast, is a vibrant city known for its beautiful beaches and year-round sunshine. Rebuilt after a devastating earthquake in 1960, Agadir is now a top destination for sun-seekers and adventure lovers alike.
